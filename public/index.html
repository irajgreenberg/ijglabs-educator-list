ijglabs is building a whitelist of educators and academic administrators who want to use the platform to teach creative coding, creative computing, computer science, generative media, AI literacy, digital art, design technology, and related courses or programs.

01

About ijglabs

ijglabs is a fully AI-mediated platform for creative computation. It supports learning and production across code, image, video, 3D, generative systems, and AI-assisted creative development. Because the platform relies on AI inference and media-generation services, there are real computational costs associated with platform use.

The project extends more than 30 years of research, development, teaching, and creative practice in creative computing. It builds on Ira Greenberg's long-standing work in creative coding, computational art, and computer science pedagogy, including his early contributions to the Processing community and his three books on creative coding and computational media.

At its core, ijglabs is based on the belief that code, algorithmic thinking, creativity, and AI literacy are now fundamental capabilities for students of all backgrounds. Creative coding originally helped make computation more accessible, expressive, and personally meaningful. ijglabs extends that lineage into the AI era.

AI is not treated as a replacement for human creativity or learning. Instead, it functions as a human-capacity multiplier: a scaffold for exploration, iteration, reflection, and deeper engagement with computational ideas.

ijglabs champions a new integrated pedagogy:

Creative Coding + AI

pedagogy / continuum

This approach connects the history of creative computation with the emerging possibilities of AI-mediated learning, giving students a powerful path into computation as both a personal and professional medium.

Who can apply

Educators may teach at any level, including K–12, community college, undergraduate, graduate, continuing education, executive education, or informal learning programs. Applicants should have an institutional affiliation and use an educational or institutional email address when possible.
